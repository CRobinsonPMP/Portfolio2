                        <h3>Project Overview</h3>
                        <p>The Mobile Ecosystem Project is a strategic initiative designed to strengthen and optimize the supply chain network supporting Airbus operations in Mobile, Alabama. As the Project Manager within the PMO, Supply Chain & Procurement team, I lead efforts to develop local supplier capabilities, create procurement forecasts, and drive investment in the regional industrial landscape.</p>
                        
                        <p>This initiative aims to reduce supply chain vulnerabilities, enhance procurement efficiency, and create a more resilient manufacturing ecosystem through strategic supplier development and data-driven decision making.</p>
                        
                        <div class="case-study-image">
                            <img src="images/supply-chain-overview.jpg" alt="Supply Chain Overview">

                    <div class="case-study-section">
                        <h3>Key Responsibilities</h3>
                        <ul>
                            <li>Leading internal customer interviews to develop comprehensive subcontracting forecasts and procurement planning strategies</li>
                            <li>Conducting supplier interviews to evaluate capabilities and encourage investment in Mobile's industrial landscape</li>
                            <li>Co-leading a cross-functional team (MFT) of engineering and procurement professionals focused on sourcing and developing local suppliers for specialized Jig & Tool products</li>
                            <li>Interfacing directly with key suppliers, including AGSE, managing the Request for Information (RFI) process</li>
                            <li>Developing data-driven reporting systems to track KPIs, supplier performance, and procurement efficiency</li>
                            <li>Designing supplier scoring models to assess performance and enhance supplier accountability</li>
                            <li>Facilitating supplier development routines to improve supply chain resilience</li>
                            <li>Implementing procurement process improvements using Lean principles and root cause analysis</li>
                        </ul>

                    <div class="case-study-section">
                        <h3>The Challenge</h3>
                        <p>When I joined the Mobile Ecosystem Project, the supply chain faced several critical challenges:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li>Limited local supplier capabilities for specialized aerospace components and tooling</li>
                            <li>Inefficient procurement processes resulting in extended lead times</li>
                            <li>Inadequate data visibility across the supply chain</li>
                            <li>Inconsistent supplier performance measurement methodologies</li>
                            <li>Fragmented stakeholder communication between engineering and procurement teams</li>
                            <li>Need for strategic investment to strengthen the regional supply ecosystem</li>
                        </ul>
                        
                        <div class="quote">
                            "The goal was to transform the Mobile supply chain from a transactional procurement model to a strategic ecosystem that drives both operational excellence and regional economic development."
                        </div>

                    <div class="case-study-section">
                        <h3>Supplier Development Methodology</h3>
                        <p>A key component of the Mobile Ecosystem Project was the development of a systematic approach to supplier evaluation and development:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li><strong>RFI Process Enhancement:</strong> Redesigned the Request for Information process to gather more meaningful capability data from potential suppliers</li>
                            <li><strong>Scoring Model Development:</strong> Created a comprehensive supplier scoring model incorporating quality, delivery, cost, and capability metrics</li>
                            <li><strong>Cross-Functional Collaboration:</strong> Established a Multi-Functional Team (MFT) bringing together engineering and procurement expertise to evaluate technical capabilities</li>
                            <li><strong>Capability Mapping:</strong> Documented current supplier capabilities against future requirements to identify investment opportunities</li>
                            <li><strong>Development Roadmaps:</strong> Created customized development plans for key suppliers with specific milestones and objectives</li>
                        </ul>

                        <p>To drive data-informed decision making, I developed several analytics-based tools and systems:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li><strong>KPI Dashboard:</strong> Designed interactive dashboards tracking critical procurement metrics including lead times, quality performance, and cost variances</li>
                            <li><strong>Subcontracting Forecast Model:</strong> Created predictive models to anticipate future procurement needs based on production schedules and historical data</li>
                            <li><strong>Supplier Performance Tracking:</strong> Implemented automated performance monitoring tools integrating data from multiple systems</li>
                            <li><strong>Investment Impact Analysis:</strong> Developed ROI models to evaluate potential supplier investments against operational benefits</li>
                            <li><strong>Process Efficiency Metrics:</strong> Established measurement systems to quantify improvements from procurement process changes</li>

                        <h3>Project Management Approach</h3>
                        <p>Successfully managing the Mobile Ecosystem Project required leveraging both traditional and agile project management methodologies:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li><strong>Hybrid PM Framework:</strong> Implemented a flexible approach combining structured milestone planning with agile sprints for rapid deliverable development</li>
                            <li><strong>Stakeholder Management:</strong> Developed comprehensive communication plans for diverse stakeholders including internal customers, suppliers, and executive leadership</li>
                            <li><strong>Risk Management:</strong> Created a proactive risk identification and mitigation framework specific to supply chain initiatives</li>
                            <li><strong>Resource Optimization:</strong> Coordinated cross-functional team resources to maximize efficiency and expertise utilization</li>
                            <li><strong>Change Management:</strong> Facilitated process transitions through structured change management frameworks focused on user adoption</li>

                    <div class="case-study-section">
                        <h3>Lessons Learned</h3>
                        <p>Throughout this ongoing project, several valuable insights have emerged:</p>
                        <ul>
                            <li><strong>Data Integration Challenges:</strong> Connecting disparate data sources required more extensive transformation than initially anticipated</li>
                            <li><strong>Cross-Functional Alignment:</strong> Early engagement of engineering stakeholders significantly improved technical evaluation processes</li>
                            <li><strong>Supplier Communication:</strong> Transparent sharing of development objectives with suppliers accelerated improvement initiatives</li>
                            <li><strong>Metric Standardization:</strong> Establishing common performance metrics across the supply base enabled more effective benchmarking</li>
                            <li><strong>Capability vs. Capacity:</strong> Finding suppliers with both technical capability and sufficient capacity proved more challenging than expected</li>
                        </ul>
                        
                        <div class="quote">
                            "The most significant insight was that supplier development is as much about relationship building as it is about technical capability assessment. Creating mutual trust and shared objectives proved essential for driving meaningful investment decisions."
